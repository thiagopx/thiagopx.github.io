Thiago M. Paixão

Thiago M. Paixão is a Brazilian researcher and Associate Professor of Computer Science at the Federal Institute of Espírito Santo (IFES). His career combines applied research and teaching in artificial intelligence, with expertise in computer vision and, more recently, natural language processing. He holds a Ph.D. in Computer Science from the Federal University of Espírito Santo (UFES), during which he worked as a visiting researcher at the École de Technologie Supérieure in Montreal, Canada.

At IFES, Thiago advises research in the Applied Computing graduate program and teaches in the Bachelor’s program in Information Systems. His current research interests include Large Language Models (LLMs), Visual Language Models (VLMs), self-supervised learning, and emotional analysis applied to mental health.

AI Projects

2024 — Present

Research Coordinator

Facial Analysis in Selfies to Identify Anxiety and Depression in Pregnant Patients

This project aims to develop an AI system to detect anxiety and depression in pregnant women by using selfies and self-assessment questionnaires to train predictive models, specifically aiming for authentic facial expression analysis outside controlled environments, as part of the Grávida Digital macroproject.

2024 — Present

Research Coordinator

Identifying Anxiety and Depression Using Facial Analysis in a Reduced Database

This research explores innovative methods to identify depression and anxiety through facial expression analysis in selfies, employing transfer learning and OpenFace for feature extraction, investigating fake smile detection, and planning to develop a mobile application to collect passive selfie data, while addressing small database challenges.

2024 — Present

Research Coordinator

Text-To-SQL Solutions for IAGROGPT: An Intelligent Virtual Assistant for Brazilian Agriculture

This project is dedicated to developing Text-to-SQL solutions for IAGROGPT, an intelligent virtual assistant specifically designed for the Brazilian agribusiness sector.

2023 — 2024

Research Coordinator

Air Writing Using Computer Vision and Machine Learning

This concluded research project centered on "Air Writing", leveraging computer vision and machine learning techniques to recognize text and detect writing intention, also developing the computer vision algorithm for electronic pens and points of interest.

2022 — 2024

Research Coordinator

Blast Furnace Temperature Prediction Using Machine Learning

This project aimed to enhance blast furnace temperature control in a steel mill using AI to predict multi-step ahead temperatures, replacing manual monitoring to reduce pollutant emissions, production costs, and production stoppages while improving pig iron quality by enabling continuous monitoring and anticipatory adjustments.

2022 — 2024

Senior AI Researcher

Visual Classifier Training Process Using Reduced Databases

This research focused on the training process of visual classifiers when utilizing reduced databases, which included enhancing classification models with data and generating databases using internet data.

2021 — Present

AI Researcher

Institute of Applied Computational Intelligence - I2CA

The I2CA's mission is to conduct research in applied computational intelligence for industry, emphasizing autonomous vehicles and intelligent systems, training specialized personnel, and fortifying the AI business ecosystem by researching Artificial Visual Cognition and developing powerful Autonomous Computational Intelligences (ICAs) through macroprojects in collaboration with various partners to create new technologies.

2020 — Present

Research Coordinator

Grávida Digital - Development of mobile-based software to identify depression, anxiety, and stress during pregnancy

The core objective of this project is to develop an algorithm to identify depression, anxiety, and stress in pregnant women by using voice biomarker technology, facial expression analysis, cell phone usage patterns, metadata, data mining, and artificial intelligence, involving partnerships with several universities.
